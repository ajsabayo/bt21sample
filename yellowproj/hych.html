How you can help


Depression is a serious disorder that affects millions of people, from young to old and from all beings. It gets in the way of everyday life, causing tremendous pain, hurting not just from the who's suffering but also affecting everyone around them.
Helping your loved one with depression can be overwhelming if you also don't take care of yourself. Start by educating yourself about depression and how to converse about it with your loved one.
As you reach out, don't forget about your emotion health, thinking about yourself is not an act of selfishness but it's a necessity. Your emotional strength will allow you to provide the ongoing support your depressed loved one needs.

  • Ways to start the conversation:

    • "I have been feeling concerned about you lately."
    "Recently, I have noticed some differences in you and wondered how you are doing."
    "I wanted to check in with you because you have seemed pretty down lately."


  • Questions you ask:

    • "When did you begin feeling like this?"
    "Did something happen that made you start feeling this way?"
    "How can I best support you right now?"
    "Have you thought about getting help?"

  • What you can say that helps:

    • "You are not alone in this. I'm here for you."
    "You may not believe it now, but the way you're feeling will change."
    "I may not be able to understand exactly how you feel,
    but I care about you and want to help."
    "When you want to give up, tell yourself you will hold on for just one more day,
    hour, minute-whatever you can manage."
    "You are important to me. Your life is important to me."
    "Tell me what I can do now to help you."


  • What you should avoid saying:

    • "It's all in your head."
    "We all go through times like this."
    "Look on the bright side."
    "You have so much to live for, why do you want to die?"
    "I can't do anything about your situation."
    "Just snap out of it."
    "What's wrong with you?"
    "Shouldn't you be better by now?"
